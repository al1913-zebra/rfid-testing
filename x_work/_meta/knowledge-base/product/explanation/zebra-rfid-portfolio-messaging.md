### Outcome Statements


**1:** Gramedia, a popular bookstore chain in Southeast Asia, was dealing with long checkout lines, manual
errors in transactions, and trouble keeping track of inventory. To tackle these challenges, they turned
to Zebraʼs RFID technology and integrated it into their self-checkout systems. This move made
checkouts faster and more accurate, saving time on inventory
management—up to 50%. It also gave staff more time to focus on offering personalized customer service,


leading to happier customers and smoother operations overall.


**2:** Fanatics, a global digital sports platform specializing in licensed sports merchandise, dramatically
improved their checkout
process and inventory management by adopting Zebra RFID technology. What used to take 6-8 hours for

inventory counts now only takes an hour, so they can do more frequent checks with fewer errors. Plus,
with fewer people needed at the checkout,
more staff can help customers on the sales floor, cutting down wait times and making the shopping
experience even better.


**3:** Boggi Milano, a global menswear retailer, partnered with Zebra to implement RFID technology,
transforming their inventory accuracy and stock management. With a 99% inventory accuracy rate, they
can now efficiently track products and orders with ease. This upgrade has not only enhanced the
customer experience but also paved the way for future innovations in their
operations.


**4:** Zebra has partnerships with more than one-third of NFL teams, providing the MotionWorks Sport
solution to track player performance during practices. By embedding RFID tags into shoulder pads,
footballs, and other equipment, Zebra enables real-time tracking of player movements, impacts,
and workloads. Zebra delivers live data during practice and valuable
information after practice for analysis. This data helps coaches and trainers optimize performance,

prevent injuries, and ensure equipment safety.
